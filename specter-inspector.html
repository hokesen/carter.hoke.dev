Specter Inspector

What Is Specter Inspector?

A 2D horror task-management and evasion game built in a custom engine, where gameplay occurs through draggable desktop-style windows.

Players navigate a hostile graveyard, manage tools, and evade specters while maintaining situational awareness through constrained windowed views.

Developed by an 8-person academic team and shipped to Steam.

My Roles

I worked as level designer and design lead, owning encounter flow, player readability, and design cohesion across gameplay systems.

Level Designer

My primary responsibility was to ensure mechanics and systems were presented in spaces that made their purpose, risk, and payoff immediately legible to players.

I designed encounter pacing around tool friction, enemy variety, and constrained visibility to keep tension high without becoming unfair.

Design Lead

I aligned design direction with our game pillars, managed designer priorities, and coordinated implementation with engineering.

I also handled recurring playtesting and user research reporting to ensure iteration decisions were evidence-backed.

Level Design Process

The process moved from Unity prototyping to custom-engine implementation, with repeated adjustments to pacing, navigation clarity, and challenge curves.

Prototype Learning

Early Unity prototypes established that complex mazes produced frustration, while broader, more directed spaces felt clearer and more engaging under window-limited visibility.

Flow Restructure

The original single gauntlet level created uneven stress and pacing. I restructured the game into three modules to better onboard mechanics and escalate tension in a controlled arc.

  • Inspector Training: movement and baseline interactions.
  • E.M.F. Training: specter detection and low-risk encounters.
  • E.M.P. Training: higher-intensity scenarios and climax.

Production & Teaming

A two-semester schedule with a custom engine required close coupling between design and engineering from prototype parity through serialized content integration.

Scope

Five programmers built the engine while three designers developed and iterated gameplay in parallel. The final game shipped with roughly 13 minutes of structured gameplay.

Engine Integration

We exported tilemaps, UI elements, enemies, and content data from Unity into our engine through JSON-based serialization.

My implementation work focused on geometry correction, collision tuning, encounter reliability, and continuous regression playtesting.

Steam Polish & Release

After academic submission, we resumed development in summer 2025 for release polish, store assets, and final platform delivery.

Following a development pause, I helped rally a small strike team to prepare the Steam launch build.

Final work included UI updates, bug fixes, trailer production, and full Steam page asset creation.

Specter Inspector released on August 15, 2025.
