Expansive Fantasy Survival

What Is Expansive Fantasy Survival?

A solo worldbuilding project focused on large-scale terrain composition, environmental storytelling, and exploration flow across a handcrafted 15k x 15k world.

Expansive Fantasy Survival is a free fantasy-themed Minecraft map built over 2.5 years.

The world was primarily sculpted in WorldPainter with custom brushes, structures, and biome-level iteration, then refined in-engine with WorldEdit and hand-authored landmarks.

The final map spans roughly 86 square miles of custom terrain.

Solo Development Process

This pipeline combined macro planning, constant in-engine review, and late-phase world polish to keep readability and exploration value high across the full map.

  • Top-down planning: Defined biome distribution, travel routes, landmarks, and map-level pacing before major terrain sculpting.
  • Terrain blocking in WorldPainter: Iterated biome borders, elevation flow, and structural placement over many production passes.
  • High-frequency playtesting: Exported and tested sections repeatedly in Minecraft to evaluate navigation clarity and visual cohesion.
  • Final polish pass: Added roads, ruins, villages, and storytelling landmarks to reduce dead space and reinforce world identity.
  • Scope closure: Finalized naming, packaging, and public release once each region met quality and cohesion goals.
